Chapter 0: A Quick Introduction

I've seen a lot of people, specially friends, claim that they like fighting games
but they never seem to be able to get into, or properly learn how to "play" them
despite the interest being there. And I perfectly understand why
Other genres of games are very straight forward, games like shootin games are
relatively easy to pick and understand, with most changing being classes
and weapons in which you can learn in five or ten minutes, and other genres like
platformers are so simple, anyone could pick and play them instantly

Fighting games, however, are their own can of worms that seemingly change completely with
each new entry, series, and even updates in general, which makes learning fighting games
a daunting task for most new players, because they see how different every game is, not only
within it's own series, but to others aswell, that they don't know where or how to start
because a lot of them think they'll have to relearn an entirely new genre once a new entry
comes out due to how much these games seem to add, change or remove mechanics with each
new entry, which ends up discouraging a lot of people from even trying to begin with

SF3:Third Strike was completely different from SF3:Second Impact, Guilty Gear Strive
is completely different from GG:Xrd and GGXXACPR (for the worse), and so on

But what a lot of new players don't know, is that despite all these games being completely
different from each other, most of them share the same foundation, so just like the other
genres, despite each game having it's own characters and mechanics to learn, once you learn
certain rules and foundations, they'll carry on to other titles aswell, and you'll be able
to learn and adapt yourself to new games easier and faster

And since some of my friends keep telling me "Wow, June, you're quite good at fighting games
and handsome too! you should probably make a guide on how to play fighting games so I can be
as smart and handsome like you one day!"

I figured out it could be a nice idea, because anyone can play fighting games, but properly
learning them while still enjoying yourself? Not so much.

Believe me or not, but I used to hate fighting games when I was younger, but after learning
a few things and taking a bit of a time with them, I learned how to love them, and if I can
learn how to love, you can too! probably! it's not guaranteed!

So now, I'll teach and show you some of the things that helped me not only learn how to enjoy
this genre, but helped me improve when it comes to playing them aswell

Buckle up, fucker, because this is...

JUNE'S FIGHTING GUIDE!

(For Fighting Games :^) )
